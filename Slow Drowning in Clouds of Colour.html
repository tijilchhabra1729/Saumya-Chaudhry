Slow Drowning in Clouds of Colours

Year

2021

Size

11 x 11 in

Medium

Digital Illustration, Painting, Clay, and Glitter

Description

It is often speculated that Homer and all his Greek contemporaries could not see the color blue — the sea for them was described as a “dark wine”.


Women in India often bound by patriarchial systems find their sexuality to be splattered in shades of blue. They are but mere Grecians in search of what words cannot explain. The expression of female sexual desires within Indian households is often considered taboo even within the contemporary world.


The colors in the artwork are a representation of female desires, voluminous (as represented by the sculptural accents), and bold. My subject in the artwork is ironic to the story I express: dressed “modestly”, and sitting coyly. Lest she would dare to, I (the society) drown my subject in colors she will never touch.
